# Drill 02 (Advanced): Tabletop Exercise Design and Facilitation

## Objective

Design and facilitate a 2-hour tabletop exercise for a fictional organization.
This advanced drill develops your ability to plan, structure, and extract learning from IR exercises.

## Your Role

You are the exercise designer and facilitator for SecurBank (regional bank, 500 employees, processes retail banking transactions).
The exercise audience: CISO, IT Manager, Head of Operations, Legal Counsel, Communications Director, SOC Manager.

## Part A: Exercise Design

Design a complete tabletop exercise including:

1. **Scenario Selection:** Choose one of the following attack scenarios:
   * Business Email Compromise targeting CFO (€500k wire fraud attempt)
   * Insider threat: disgruntled sysadmin exfiltrates customer data before resignation
   * Supply chain compromise: SaaS HR provider breached, SecurBank credentials exposed

1. **Three Scenario Injects:** For your chosen scenario, write three staged injects that:
   * Inject 1: Introduces the initial incident (incomplete information)
   * Inject 2: Adds complications (spread of incident OR external pressure)
   * Inject 3: Forces a decision that has no perfect answer (ethical/legal dilemma)

1. **Discussion Questions:** Write 2 questions for each inject that test specific IR plan elements.

1. **Hot Wash Template:** Design the post-exercise debrief format including:
   * Questions to identify plan gaps
   * Questions to identify communication failures
   * Questions to generate action items

## Part B: Facilitate the Exercise (In-class Activity)

During the practical session, facilitate a 30-minute mini tabletop with fellow students taking different organizational roles.

**Facilitator responsibilities:**

* Present each inject clearly
* Manage time
* Probe answers with follow-up questions
* Document gaps and disagreements

## Part C: Gap Analysis Report

After the exercise, write a gap analysis report identifying:

* 3 IR plan gaps exposed by the exercise
* 2 communication failures observed
* 3 prioritized action items to address the gaps

## Part D: Reflection

Answer:

1. What surprised you about the exercise participants' responses?
1. What is harder to simulate in a tabletop vs. a real incident?
1. How often should tabletop exercises be conducted? Justify.
1. What would you change about the exercise design for next time?

## Evaluation Criteria

Your exercise design will be evaluated on:

* Realism of the scenario
* Clarity of inject information
* Whether injects reveal actual plan gaps
* Quality of discussion questions (do they uncover problems?)
* Action items are specific and measurable

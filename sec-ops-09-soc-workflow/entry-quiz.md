# Entry Quiz: Session 09 — SOC Workflow and Automation

**Format:** 6 multiple-choice questions (1 correct answer each)

**Purpose:** Assess prerequisite knowledge before the session

**Level:** Easy — beginner accessible

---

### Question 1

An analyst receives 200 security alerts during an 8-hour shift and manages to review 80 of them.
The remaining 120 are ignored.
What is this phenomenon called?

A) Threat overload
B) **Alert fatigue** ✓
C) Detection gap
D) Signal flooding

*Alert fatigue is when analysts become desensitized or overwhelmed by alert volume and stop reviewing all alerts, often causing real threats to be missed.*

### Question 5

A Tier 1 analyst has been investigating an alert for 25 minutes and cannot determine if it is a true positive or false positive.
According to standard SOC procedure, what should they do?

A) Close the alert as a false positive to meet the SLA
B) Continue investigating until they are certain
C) Delete the alert from the queue and re-check tomorrow
D) **Escalate to a Tier 2 analyst** ✓

*When an L1 analyst cannot resolve uncertainty within the SLA window, the correct action is escalation — not forced closure.*

---

### Question 6

In a SOC RACI matrix, a Tier 1 analyst is listed as **R** (Responsible) for alert triage and **I** (Informed) for threat hunting.
What does this mean in practice?

A) Tier 1 must approve all threat hunting activities before they begin
B) **Tier 1 performs alert triage independently and is notified of threat hunting outcomes but does not participate** ✓
C) Tier 1 is responsible for threat hunting if no Tier 3 analyst is available
D) Tier 1 must consult Tier 3 before closing any alert

*In a RACI matrix, R (Responsible) = does the work; I (Informed) = receives updates but does not act.
A Tier 1 analyst "I" on threat hunting simply receives results — they are not involved in execution.*

## Short Answer Questions

**Question 1:** What Splunk query would you use to count the number of failed SSH login attempts grouped by source IP address?
Write the SPL query.

*Expected answer:*

```spl
index=main sourcetype=linux_secure "Failed password"
| rex "from (?<src_ip>\d+\.\d+\.\d+\.\d+)"
| stats count by src_ip
| sort -count
```

*(Accept any functionally equivalent query that extracts src_ip and counts failures)*

*Expected answer:*

* Classify as True Positive / Critical
* Escalate to Tier 2 immediately
* Recommend isolating the affected machine from the network

**Question 4:** Name three KPIs commonly used to measure SOC performance and briefly describe what each measures.

*Expected answer:*

* **MTTD (Mean Time to Detect)**: Average time between when an incident occurs and when it is first detected.
* **MTTR (Mean Time to Respond)**: Average time between detection and containment/resolution.
* **False Positive Rate**: Percentage of alerts generated by the SIEM that turn out to not be real threats.

*(Also accept: Alert volume, escalation rate, MTTI)*

---

**Question 5:** What is the purpose of a SOAR platform in a SOC, and how does it differ from a SIEM?

*Expected answer:*

* SIEM: Collects, normalizes, and correlates log data to generate alerts. Focused on detection and visibility.
* SOAR: Automates response workflows and orchestrates actions across multiple tools. Focused on response and efficiency.
* Key difference: SIEM detects and alerts; SOAR acts and automates.

## Long Answer Questions

**Question 6:** You are a Tier 1 analyst and you receive the following alert:

> *"User jsmith attempted to log in from IP 203.0.113.100 (geolocation: North Korea) at 03:15 AM. 15 failed attempts followed by 1 successful login. The user then accessed the HR database and downloaded 3,000 records."*

Describe your complete triage process, including: your classification, severity assessment, actions taken, and what information you would include in your escalation to Tier 2.
(150-250 words)

*Expected answer should include:*

* **Classification**: True Positive
* **Severity**: Critical
* **Triage steps**: Verify the login from threat intel (North Korea IP), confirm access to HR database, check if jsmith has any legitimate reason for 3AM access from North Korea (likely none)
* **Immediate containment actions**: Disable jsmith account, block IP at firewall, preserve HR database access logs
* **Escalation note**: Include timeline of events, attacker IP, data accessed, suspected MITRE techniques (T1078 Valid Accounts, T1083 File Discovery, T1048 Exfiltration)
* **Escalation urgency**: Immediate — potential data breach of 3,000 HR records

**Question 7:** Explain the concept of "alert fatigue" in a SOC environment.
What causes it, what are its consequences, and what strategies can a SOC implement to mitigate it?
Provide at least 3 specific mitigation strategies with justification.
(200-300 words)

*Expected answer should include:*

* **Definition**: Alert fatigue is when analysts become desensitized to alerts due to high volume, especially high false positive rates
* **Causes**: Overly broad detection rules, poorly tuned thresholds, lack of context enrichment, understaffed SOC
* **Consequences**: Missed real threats, analyst burnout and turnover, slower MTTD/MTTR, increased breach risk
* **Mitigation strategies** (any 3 of):
  1. **Rule tuning**: Regularly review and refine SIEM detection rules to reduce false positives
  1. **SOAR automation**: Automate handling of low-risk repetitive alerts (e.g., auto-close known benign patterns)
  1. **Alert prioritization**: Implement scoring systems so critical alerts always surface first
  1. **Context enrichment**: Automatically enrich alerts with threat intel data before they reach analysts
  1. **Staffing**: Ensure adequate staffing levels; rotate analysts to prevent burnout
  1. **Metric tracking**: Monitor false positive rates and target continuous reduction
